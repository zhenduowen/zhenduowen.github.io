I am currently a second-year PhD student at the Department of Information Engineering in CUHK, where I have the privilege to be advised by [Prof. Amin Gohari](https://www.ie.cuhk.edu.hk/faculty/gohari-amin/). Prior to this, I earned my master's degree from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at UC San Diego, where I worked with Prof. Alon Orlitsky. Before that, I graduated from the Department of Statistics at CUHK-Shenzhen with first-class honors in 2022.

My research interests lie at the intersection of theory and application, including information theory, learning theory, statistical estimation, algorithm design, and data-driven methodologies. Within my research areas, theoretical work often begins by identifying fundamental properties of complex systems and proceeds to develop characterizations, asymptotic results, and impossibility bounds. By contrast, applied research is frequently more problem-specific, relying on probabilistic methods to uncover patterns and guide practical solutions.

> “The development of Western science is based on two great achievements: the invention of the formal logical system in Euclidean geometry by the Greek philosophers, and the discovery of the possibility of finding causal relationships through systematic experiment during the Renaissance.” (Albert Einstein, 1953)

In general, I like abstract yet transferable concepts that arise from practical problems and can, in turn, inform principled approaches to real-world challenges.
