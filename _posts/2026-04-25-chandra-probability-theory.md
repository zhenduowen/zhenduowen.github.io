This course offered at CUHK IE originates from Prof. Amir Dembo's course at Stanford and Prof. S.R.S. Varadhan's course at New York University. I also find Billingsley's book and Durrett's book good sources for practicing measure theory (before independence) and probability theory (after independence). David William's book "Probability with Martingales" gives a good perspective for understanding Martingales, a power tool constructed on Conditional Expectation, very useful in estimation, describing current state of the problem we are investigating using sigma-algebras, and asymptotic analysis of time series and processes. A lot of analysis is also covered in this course, along with elementary exposure to set theory.

These notes are annotated version from Prof. Nair's notes. Typos could exist.
